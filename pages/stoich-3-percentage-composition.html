Stoichiometry 3 — Percentage Composition by Mass

At a glance

Method flow — percentage composition by mass

Percentage composition by mass

The percentage composition can be calculated from the formula of a compound. In the formula of a compound we can see how many moles of each element are present. We can then express the mass as a percentage of the whole molecule.

% of element = (mass of element in compound / molar mass of compound) × 100%

Example — methanal

Methanal, HCHO, is used as a preservative for biological specimens. Calculate the percentage by mass of each element in methanal.

Method: From the formula of methanal we can see that there are 2 moles of H, 1 mole of C and 1 mole of O in 1 mole of HCHO.

Molar mass of HCHO = 30 g

% H = (2 × 1 / 30) × 100 = 6.67%

% C = (1 × 12 / 30) × 100 = 40%

% O = (1 × 16 / 30) × 100 = 53.33%

Worked question — ammonium ion in ammonium sulfate

Question: Calculate the percentage by mass of ammonium ion, NH4+, in ammonium sulfate, (NH4)2SO4. (LCH 1986)

Method:

Formula of ammonium sulfate = (NH4)2SO4

Mr of (NH4)2SO4 = 2(14) + 8(1) + 32 + 4(16) = 132

Mass due to 2NH4 = 2(14) + 8(1) = 36

% NH4+ = (36 / 132) × 100 = 27.27%
