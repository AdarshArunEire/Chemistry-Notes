Organic Chemistry 2 — Natural Gas, Crude Oil, Fractional Distillation, Petrol, Ethene and Ethyne

At a glance

This section covers crude oil and fractional distillation, natural gas, petrol and octane number, anti-knock methods, catalytic converters, and the preparation and properties of ethene and ethyne.

Crude oil = mixture of hydrocarbons Fractional distillation separates by boiling point Branched / cyclic hydrocarbons raise octane number Ethene and ethyne are unsaturated

Crude oil and fractional distillation

Crude oil is a fossil fuel formed from the decomposition of dead organisms over millions of years. It is a thick, black, viscous, pungent liquid and is of little direct use until it is separated into fractions.

Fractional distillation – separation of a mixture of liquids by exploiting differences in boiling point.
  • Crude oil is heated strongly and fed into a fractionating tower.
  • The tower is hotter at the bottom and cooler at the top.
  • Heavy fractions condense lower down; lighter fractions rise higher before condensing.

Natural gas and petrol

Natural gas is a mixture of hydrocarbons, usually methane, ethane, propane and butane. In Europe, propane and butane are sold as LPG (liquefied petroleum gas). Mercaptans are added to natural gas because natural gas itself is odourless.

Octane number – the measure of the tendency of a fuel to resist auto-ignition.

Long straight-chain hydrocarbons are more likely to knock. Petrol rich in short-chain, branched, cyclic or aromatic hydrocarbons burns more smoothly.

Reference fuels: 2,2,4-trimethylpentane (iso-octane) is given octane number 100, while heptane is the poor anti-knock reference.

Methods used to improve petrol quality

  • Isomerisation: converts straight-chain hydrocarbons into branched isomers.
  • Dehydrocyclisation: converts straight-chain hydrocarbons into ring compounds, often then to aromatic compounds.
  • Catalytic cracking: breaks long-chain molecules into shorter molecules that are in greater demand.
  • Oxygenates: alcohols or ethers added to petrol to improve performance and reduce harmful emissions.

Catalytic converters in car exhausts change harmful gases such as carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides and unburned hydrocarbons into less harmful gases.

Preparation and properties of ethene

C₂H₅OH → C₂H₄ + H₂O

Ethene is prepared in the lab by dehydrating ethanol using aluminium oxide as the dehydrating agent / catalyst.

  • Glass wool holds the ethanol in place and soaks it up.
  • Heat is applied at the aluminium oxide.
  • Remove the delivery tube from the water before stopping heating to prevent suck-back.

Properties of ethene: colourless gas, sweetish smell, insoluble in water, burns with a luminous flame and small amount of smoke.

Tests for unsaturation:

  • Bromine water: red-brown → colourless.
  • Acidified / dilute potassium manganate(VII): purple → colourless.
C₂H₄ + Br₂ → C₂H₄Br₂

Preparation and properties of ethyne

CaC₂ + 2H₂O → C₂H₂ + Ca(OH)₂

Ethyne is prepared by reacting calcium dicarbide with water. Calcium dicarbide is a greyish solid and the water must be added slowly.

  • The first gas jar is discarded because it mainly contains displaced air.
  • Impurities such as phosphine and hydrogen sulfide may be removed by bubbling the gas through acidified copper(II) sulfate solution.
  • Ethyne is used for cutting and welding because it burns very hot in oxygen.

Properties of ethyne: colourless gas when pure, insoluble in water, very smoky luminous flame in air, decolourises bromine water and potassium manganate(VII).

End-of-chapter practice

Leaving Cert style

Why are mercaptans added to natural gas? Name one environmental concern linked to methane in the atmosphere.

Show answer
  • Mercaptans: added because natural gas is odourless, so leaks can be detected.
  • Environmental concern: methane is a greenhouse gas and contributes to global warming / the enhanced greenhouse effect.

Leaving Cert style

What is meant by the octane number of a fuel? Name the two reference hydrocarbons used to assign octane numbers.

Show answer
  • Octane number: the measure of the tendency of a fuel to resist auto-ignition.
  • Reference hydrocarbons: heptane and 2,2,4-trimethylpentane (iso-octane).

Leaving Cert style

Give two structural features that increase the octane number of a hydrocarbon.

Show answer
  • short chain length
  • high degree of branching
  • cyclic or aromatic character

Leaving Cert style

What is the function of the glass wool in the preparation of ethene?

Show answer

Answer: it soaks up the ethanol and holds it in place at the end of the boiling tube.

Leaving Cert style

State and explain two safety precautions in the preparation of ethene.

Show answer
  • Remove the delivery tube from the water before stopping heating: prevents suck-back into the hot tube.
  • Keep the gas away from flames: ethene is flammable.
  • Wear goggles / use tongs: reduces risk from hot glass / splashes.

Leaving Cert style

What does it mean to say that ethene or ethyne is unsaturated? Give one chemical test.

Show answer
  • Unsaturated: contains a multiple bond.
  • Test: bromine water is decolourised.
  • Alternative: acidified / dilute KMnO₄ is decolourised.

Leaving Cert style

What is the most noticeable difference between the flames of ethene and ethyne?

Show answer

Answer: ethyne burns with a smokier / sootier / more luminous flame than ethene.

Leaving Cert style

What process converts long-chain hydrocarbons into shorter molecules that are in greater demand?

Show answer

Answer: catalytic cracking.
