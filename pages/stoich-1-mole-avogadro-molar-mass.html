Stoichiometry

Stoichiometry 1 — Mole, Avogadro, Molar Mass

At a glance

This section introduces the mole, Avogadro’s constant, molar mass, molar volume at s.t.p. and the standard conversions between grams, moles, particles and gas volume.

moles = mass ÷ Mr mass = moles × Mr particles = moles × 6.0 × 1023 volume at s.t.p. = moles × 22.4 L

The mole

Mole – the amount of a substance that contains Avogadro’s number of particles.
Avogadro’s number – 6.0 × 1023 particles per mole.

The word particles can mean atoms, molecules or ions, depending on the substance in the question.

Molar mass and molar volume

To work with the mole, you must be able to move comfortably between:

  • grams ↔ moles
  • particles ↔ moles
  • gas volume at s.t.p. ↔ moles
ConversionFormula
mass → molesmoles = mass ÷ Mr
moles → massmass = moles × Mr
moles → particlesparticles = moles × 6.0 × 1023
particles → molesmoles = particles ÷ 6.0 × 1023
moles → gas volume at s.t.p.volume = moles × 22.4 L
gas volume at s.t.p. → molesmoles = volume ÷ 22.4 L
Important: 22.4 L applies only at standard temperature and pressure. If the question says room temperature and pressure, use the gas-law information given in the question.

Worked examples

Worked example

How many moles are in 5.0 g of calcium carbonate, CaCO₃?

Show solution
  • Mr(CaCO₃) = 40 + 12 + 3(16) = 100.
  • Moles = 5.0 ÷ 100 = 0.05 mol.

Worked example

What is the mass of 0.25 mol of carbon dioxide?

Show solution
  • Mr(CO₂) = 12 + 2(16) = 44.
  • Mass = 0.25 × 44 = 11 g.

Worked example

How many atoms are there in 1.12 L of hydrogen sulfide at s.t.p.?

Show solution
  • Moles of H₂S = 1.12 ÷ 22.4 = 0.05 mol.
  • Molecules of H₂S = 0.05 × 6.0 × 1023 = 3.0 × 1022.
  • Atoms per molecule = 3.
  • Total atoms = 9.0 × 1022.

Worked example

What volume at s.t.p. is occupied by 0.35 g of ethene, C₂H₄?

Show solution
  • Mr(C₂H₄) = 28.
  • Moles = 0.35 ÷ 28 = 0.0125 mol.
  • Volume at s.t.p. = 0.0125 × 22.4 = 0.28 L.

Exam-style practice

Leaving Cert style

How many electrons are there in 2.3 g of sodium metal?

Show answer
  • Moles of Na = 2.3 ÷ 23 = 0.10 mol.
  • Atoms of Na = 0.10 × 6.0 × 1023 = 6.0 × 1022.
  • Electrons per atom = 11.
  • Total electrons = 6.6 × 1023.

Leaving Cert style

How many iron atoms are needed to supply 0.014 g of iron?

Show answer
  • Moles of Fe = 0.014 ÷ 56 = 2.5 × 10−4 mol.
  • Atoms = 2.5 × 10−4 × 6.0 × 1023 = 1.5 × 1020 atoms.

Leaving Cert style

How many iron atoms are in 0.0024 g of iron in a 30 g serving of cornflakes?

Show answer
  • Moles of Fe = 0.0024 ÷ 56 = 4.29 × 10−5 mol.
  • Atoms = 4.29 × 10−5 × 6.0 × 1023 ≈ 2.57 × 1019 atoms.

Leaving Cert style

How many neutrons are there in 0.14 g of carbon-14?

Show answer
  • Moles of C-14 = 0.14 ÷ 14 = 0.01 mol.
  • Atoms of C-14 = 0.01 × 6.0 × 1023 = 6.0 × 1021.
  • Neutrons per atom = 8.
  • Total neutrons = 4.8 × 1022.

Leaving Cert style

What mass of carbon is contained in a diamond containing 18.3 moles of carbon?

Show answer

Answer: mass = 18.3 × 12 = 219.6 g.
