Organic Chemistry 7 — Elimination, Redox, Reduction and Organic Synthesis

At a glance

Redox map of alcohols

Acidity support

Ethanol: very weak acid, only reacts as an acid with very reactive metals such as sodium.

Carboxylic acids: the carbonyl group pulls electron density away from the O–H bond by the inductive effect, helping the proton leave. The resulting carboxylate ion is stabilised because the negative charge is spread over the group.

Notes

Elimination reactions

Definition – An elimination reaction is one where a small molecule is removed from a larger molecule, leaving a double bond in one of the product molecules.

The first example on the course is dehydration of an alcohol to form an alkene and water. Ethanol is dehydrated using aluminium oxide as the dehydrating agent to produce ethene. The second example is the second stage in the preparation of PVC, where 1,2-dichloroethane is converted to chloroethene and hydrogen chloride.

C₂H₅OH → C₂H₄ + H₂O    |    C₂H₄Cl₂ → C₂H₃Cl + HCl

Redox reactions of alcohols

These reactions involve oxidation and reduction. An alcohol is oxidised and the oxidising agent, sodium dichromate, is reduced. The colour change during the preparations is orange to green because dichromate is reduced to chromium(III).

  • Primary alcohol → aldehyde → carboxylic acid
  • Secondary alcohol → ketone

The notes use the examples ethanol → ethanal → ethanoic acid and propan-2-ol → propanone.

Important point: aldehydes are easily oxidised on to carboxylic acids, but ketones are not.

Laboratory apparatus used in the course: preparation of an aldehyde by distillation, preparation of a ketone by distillation, and preparation of a carboxylic acid by reflux.

Social and applied aspects

  • Breathalyser: sodium dichromate crystals are orange. Ethanol in the breath is oxidised and the dichromate is reduced, so the crystals turn green.
  • Human body: ethanol is oxidised to ethanal in the liver, and ethanal is further oxidised to carbon dioxide and water.
  • Fuel additive: in some countries ethanol is added to petrol as an oxygenate.

Reduction

Carboxylic acids are reduced to aldehydes, which can be further reduced to primary alcohols. Hydrogen is the reagent and a nickel catalyst is used. Ketones are reduced to secondary alcohols using hydrogen and a nickel catalyst.

RCOOH + 2H₂ → RCHO → RCH₂OH    |    propanone + H₂ → propan-2-ol

Combustion of alcohols

When an alcohol undergoes combustion, the products are carbon dioxide and water. This chapter includes practice prompts to write balanced equations for ethanol and propan-1-ol combustion.

Non-flammability of fully halogenated alkanes

Alkanes whose hydrogen atoms have been replaced by halogens are not very combustible. The notes say they are used as flame retardants on fabrics and in fire extinguishers because they do not allow combustion and are denser than air.

Reactions as acids

The two homologous series studied that can act as acids are alcohols and carboxylic acids.

Ethanol and sodium

Ethanol reacts with sodium to produce sodium ethoxide and hydrogen gas. The hydrogen of the hydroxyl group is polar, allowing the hydrogen to be replaced by sodium. Ethanol is an extremely weak acid, weaker than water.

2C₂H₅OH + 2Na → 2C₂H₅ONa + H₂

Why carboxylic acids are acids

The carbonyl group attracts electrons to itself, leaving the carbonyl carbon electron-poor. This pulls electrons away from the O–H bond. The notes call this the inductive effect, and it allows the proton to be donated. When the proton leaves, the carboxylate ion forms; the equal bond lengths and spread of negative charge make it extra stable.

Reactions of ethanoic acid

  • Ethanoic acid + magnesium → magnesium ethanoate + hydrogen
  • Ethanoic acid + sodium hydroxide → sodium ethanoate + water
  • Ethanoic acid + sodium carbonate → sodium ethanoate + water + carbon dioxide
Test for carboxylic acids: carbon dioxide released from the carbonate reaction turns limewater milky.

Organic synthesis

Organic synthesis involves making organic compounds from simpler materials using several steps and intermediate compounds. Bonds are broken and new bonds are formed. The notes say you should be able to work through reaction schemes of up to three conversions.

A useful example is synthesising ethanoic acid from ethene: first hydrate ethene to ethanol, then oxidise ethanol with acidified sodium dichromate to ethanoic acid.

C₂H₄ + H₂O → C₂H₅OH    then    C₂H₅OH + 2[O] → CH₃COOH + H₂O
